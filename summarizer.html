  • The magnetic North (N) and South (S) poles create a stationary magnetic field in the stator of a DC motor.
  • This field interacts with the current flowing through the rotating armature (rotor), generating a force perpendicular to both the magnetic field and current directions.
  • This force produces torque, causing the rotor to turn and convert electrical energy into mechanical energy continuously with help from the commutator and brushes.
